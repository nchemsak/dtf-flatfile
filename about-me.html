About Me.

Hi and welcome to Different Than Fitness, hosted by me, Lacey. I appreciate you taking the time to stop by and learn more about me and my continuing journey to better living through diet, mindfulness, and DDP Yoga.

My struggle with weight, food, and body issues has been life-long. Like so many, I lived in the cyclic hamster wheel that was losing weight here and gaining a little back there. None of that was a top concern or priority until a few years ago. I left a job I loved in pursuit of broader horizons. Unfortunately, those horizons lead to crushing defeat and disappointment. I then found myself out of work for the first time since I was 14. Disappointment. Next came a wonderful opportunity with an incredible set of people. I still felt empty inside. My identity had been linked to my occupation and employment for over 10 years. I started to realize that living to work would no longer be my lot in life. Disappointment. I was forced to face some very difficult and unwanted truths. The emptiness (and disappointment) grew. Unable to cope, I continued to fill this void with food, alcohol, and an occasional workout. I tried to make myself believe I was doing all I could live my life at 90%, but I was lying to myself.

My “enough” moment came mid-2017 when I reached out to the DDP Yoga community in search of the support and love, for which they are infamous. That choice turned my life around and rearranged priorities in my life. I have lost over 20lbs using DDPY and following a diet that includes gluten-free, plant-based, dairy-free principles. I also began the path to DDPY certification. It was here I discovered my true passion lies in helping others realize their potential and “owning their lives”. None of this would be possible without the leadership and inspiration of Diamond Dallas Page and the DDP Yoga program. My goal is to lead, inspire, mentor, and pay-it-forward to honor his life’s work.

Wishing you all the best luck on your journey, wherever you are and wherever it takes you. It’s never too late or too early to recreate and reinvent yourself and your story. (That’s why the ouroboros is in my logo.)

Lacey
